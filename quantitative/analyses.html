UK exposure to economic coercion

Economic coercion from other countries is a threat to UK resilience. Leading economics organisations are increasingly concerned about the problem. It's not hard to see why, the fallout from the Liberation Day Tariffs have been severe.

One way to identify risk is to find goods for which the UK relies on a single main supplier with few alternatives.

First, who are the UK's main suppliers?

Let's focus on the UK's largest import market, China (by replicating the code linked below we could look at the US or any other trading partner).

The UK is reliant on China for umbrellas and China makes up a large share of total global exports of umbrellas. Losing access to umbrellas would be sad but might not be a catastrophe. Man-made filaments are more concerning, but at least it doesn't make up a large share of the UK's imports. Lastly, China makes up a large share of the UK's imports of tobacco, but thankfully other countries may trade with the UK if China stops.

New Zealand Crime

Something that keeps coming up is the amount of crime around Wellington. People claim that crime increased in the city.

I decided to take a look at reported crime in Wellington, based on Police NZ data. It is important to interpret these cautiously; these figures reflect only reported crimes. The true level of crime is likely higher, and some types of crime are more likely to go unreported than others.

Interestingly, there is no clear sign that reported crime in Wellington has increased over 2022-2025.

I include Auckland City crime for comparison (below). There has been a clear increase in the amount of reported theft over the same period.
