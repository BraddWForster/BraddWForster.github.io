Brexit's expected and unexpected effects

It's been four years since Brexit.
Enough time has passed to observe the short- to medium-term effects on the United Kingdom.
The full extent of the cost is of great interest to politicians, economists, media, and the public.
Several London School of Economics Professors have taken it upon themselves to answer this question.
Their work, suggests:

  • sharp drop in both exports and imports with the EU for the average UK firm
  • many exporters and importers stopped trading with the UK completely

Reference:
Freeman, R., Garofalo, M., Longoni, E., Manova, K., Mari, R., Prayer, T., & Sampson, T. (2024). Deep integration and trade: UK firms in the wake of Brexit.
